# Workflow Hypotheses

- Hypothesis: Requiring insight capture only after meaningful tasks will keep the knowledge base higher-signal than requiring updates after every trivial action.
  Why it seems plausible: The repo already distinguishes meaningful changes from low-risk churn in its testing and documentation rules.
  Confirmation count: 1
  What would confirm it: Several future tasks produce concise, useful entries without generating large amounts of low-value noise.
  What would contradict it: Important lessons are repeatedly missed because the trigger was too narrow.

- Hypothesis: The `workflows` domain should stay small and mostly capture repo-operating patterns, while product and runtime learnings should move into domains like `discord`, `permissions`, or `retrieval`.
  Why it seems plausible: Mixing operational process lessons with runtime behavior would make domain retrieval less precise.
  Confirmation count: 1
  What would confirm it: Future knowledge entries cluster naturally into clearer product or system domains.
  What would contradict it: Workflow and runtime learnings remain tightly coupled and need to live together for context.
